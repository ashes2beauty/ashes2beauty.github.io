How to clean your wedding rings

DIY | 28 September 2016

I hardly ever take off my wedding rings. Truth be told, I even do gardening with them.   The problem is that my rings have all sorts of openings and crevices where little pieces of gunk go live in. I have tried cleaning it with a toothbrush, but it alone cannot remove the gunk out of the small openings. My rings also tend to get dull due to all sorts of things getting on there- cream, butter, makeup, etc.

At one stage my ring broke and I had to take it to the jewelers to get fixed and they also cleaned it. The difference in how my rings looked was amazing to me. It felt like I had a brand new, sparkly friend! But, the problem is that cleaning the jewelry at the jeweler comes at quite a steep (in my humble opinion) cost- especially if you consider how often you would need to take it in for a clean.

I recently found this article at Living with Landyn. There the author of the blog shares a ‘potion’ for cleaning real gold/ diamond/ platinum jewelry. She received this potion from a jeweler.

Here are the ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup window cleaner
  • a few drops teaspoon dish washing detergent

The method:

  • Mix the ingredients in a glass, microwaveable container (a container that has a lid).
  • Microwave the ingredients for 1 minute (no lid on, no jewelry inserted)
  • Add jewelry to soak for 5-10 minutes. You can let it soak overnight for a deep clean.
  • Gently scrub the jewelry with a soft bristle toothbrush.
  • Rinse jewelry in lukewarm water.
  • Dry with a paper towel.

You can reuse the solution, hence the lid for the microwaveable container. Whenever you need to clean something, just pop it in the microwave, soak and then store again. Over time the solution will collect more gunk residue. When it gets too much, throw out the solution and mix a new batch.

The results are amazing, it does not take much effort and you probably already have all the required ingredients on hand.
