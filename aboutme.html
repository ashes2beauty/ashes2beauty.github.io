About Me

Want to learn how to get more bang for your buck, become the best you and beat the clock with household chores? Great, that is what I aim to help people with here. Ashes2Beauty was launched in September 2016 as a community for young women and housewives (or anyone else who is interested). I am here to discover and share with you:
  • How to save money with all sorts of DIY
  • Recipes that you will love
  • Tips on improving your health and beauty
I am still learning, too, but I love to share things as I discover them. When I discover them, I try and test and then label. If I label them red- the results were horrible; orange- the results were okay; green- the results were fantastic! Then you can generally see if something is going to work or not. Please feel free to share your own results with me in the comments section of each blog post. Stick with me, won't you?

So, who am I?

Pleased to meet you. Currently based in Pretoria, South Africa, Ashes 2 Beauty was started by Esteé. I got married about a year ago to a wonderful and loving husband. I soon discovered that I have many skills lacking (especially in the cleaning department); my recipe repertoire is very small and I do not really know how to host guests. Two months after we got married I started my first job. It was very hectic and we basically lived off takeaways and our house was a mess. Working 6 days a week and traveling far left me too tired to cook and clean. I realized the value of quick dinners and speedy cleans.

Seven months later I resigned, and without a job, I started staying at home. We are very blessed, but with only one income and expenses to pay, budgeting our monthly expenses became a real priority. As I began researching these various things I started sharing the information with close friends and family. I soon realized that I want to share the information with anyone willing to listen - and I really needed a hobby other than cleaning - I decided to start a blog. I chose the name Ashes 2 Beauty, because I want to show you how to turn things that look like a mess (ashes) to you, into something that makes you happy and rewards you (beauty). Let's be friends, yes?
